# Core problems this system solves

This AI gifting experience (inspired by Elfster) is built around **three problems** people hit when they shop for gifts using **everyday language**—for example, *“something nice for my sister who loves baking, under $40.”*

---

## 1. Budget-constrained gift discovery

### What users struggle with

- Hard to guess what “reasonable” costs for a category without clicking many links.
- Easy to fall in love with ideas that are **too expensive**, or to settle for **boring** options just to stay cheap.
- Filtering by price across many sites or lists feels slow and repetitive.

### What the system does

- Reads the budget from what the user says (or asks for it if missing).
- Surfaces ideas that **fit the price range** so browsing stays focused and less frustrating.

---

## 2. Interest and personality-based gift matching

### What users struggle with

- Generic gift lists ignore **who someone really is**—their hobbies, humor, values, and style.
- Hard to connect “they like X” to **specific, believable** gift ideas without deep product knowledge.

### What the system does

- Picks up **interests, vibe, and relationship** from natural language.
- Suggests gifts that **line up with that person**, not one-size-fits-all picks.

---

## 3. Occasion, group, and seasonal gift planning

### What users struggle with

- Different rules for **office vs. family vs. friends** (tone, cost, appropriateness).
- **Seasons and holidays** add time pressure and crowd the mental list.
- **Group gifts or exchanges** need ideas that work for “someone you barely know” or a whole household.

### What the system does

- Uses **occasion, group type, and timing** from the user’s message to steer suggestions.
- Helps users plan for **the situation**, not only “a random gift off a shelf.”
